Introducing the Song Network

Welcome stranger! Prepare to dive into the world of songs, similar songs, and similar songs of similar songs of songs that share the same tags and use the same words. Based on the Million Song Dataset we created a network of songs, tags and words that tells a thousand stories about songs without a single sound.

95224

Songs

Songs have properties like artist, title and release year. They can have a similarity relationship between each other, which holds a score indicating how similar two songs are, if at all. Around a third of all songs have full information about tags, words, year, artist and title.

106246

Tags

Tags are user defined phrases that describe a song. These might be a genre like "rock" or descriptions of something, the tagged songs may have in common like "sad" or "title is a full sentence".

5000

Words

We are taking a network-based bag of words approach. Our database contains the 5000 most common words across the songs where lyrics are available. A song is connected to a word with the "contains" relationship that also holds the number of times the word occurs in that song.

The song graph

Why are songs building a network you ask? Songs, Tags, Words, what the heck? Have a look below! This graph illustrates the basic structure of our song graph with a small example. Songs () can have directed similarity relationships between each other. These relationships form a very dense graph, so that the giant connected component contains over 90% of all nodes. As you can see, you even find a path between Taylor Swift and Cannibal Corpse.

Furthermore, our graph contains user defined tags () and lyric words (). Tags are added to songs by users and often contain genre information. The collection of words a song is linked to forms a bag-of-words description of the lyrics. Obviously, the intermediate nodes have themselves multiple similarity, tag and word relationships, which are ommitted here to keep it simple. Drag the nodes and have fun while you are preparing to dive into the world of music.

The Tags & Similarity graphs

Degree distribution of song similarity

This is the degree distribution of both incoming and outgoing similarity relationships for all nodes. This network does not seem to scale freely as the maximum degree seems to have an upper bound.

Degree distribution of tags

The degree distribution of tags (how many songs are tagged with a particular tag) closely follows a power law. As the network size increases, new nodes will show a preference to be tagged by tags with high degree.

Year distribution of the songs

Looking at the year distribution graph we find that most of the songs are from the 21st century with decreasing amounts the older the songs are. The observed years range from 1926 to 2011 with 2007 being the year with most songs.

10 most popular Tags

Here we can see the tags that have the most songs. We can see that the most popular tags almost exclusively describe musical genres.
